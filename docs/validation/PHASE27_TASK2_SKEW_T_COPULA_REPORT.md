## Method

X = gamma·W + sqrt(W)·Z, W ~ InvGamma(df/2, df/2), Z ~ N(0, Sigma), drawn from the
IDENTICAL rng stream as the symmetric simulator so **gamma = 0 recovers the frozen t
EXACTLY** (recovery deviation 0.00e+00 ≤ 1e-9). The univariate GH skew-t marginal CDF is
evaluated by Gauss-Laguerre quadrature (gamma > 0) with an exact Student-t short-circuit
at gamma = 0; margins stay uniform so the frozen empirical margins are untouched.

## Material finding (disclosed)

The leakage-free fit pins gamma at its lower boundary (gamma_hat=6.24e-05): the realised average pairwise UPPER-tail co-exceedance of the standalone loss vectors (0.1518 at p=0.90) is BELOW the frozen symmetric-t level (0.2360) and shows no upper-tail asymmetry, so the skew-t asymmetry lever (which can only RAISE the upper tail) does not activate. The skew-t at gamma_hat is economically identical to the frozen t (delta +0.01%, within the quadrature-PIT tolerance). The mechanism IS correctly implemented and powerful (the disclosed gamma grid lifts the component SCR to 54600 at gamma=1.0 and overshoots the nested 46638.9), but the copula-FORM residual (6,120.2; 91.9% of the 14.29% nested gap) is NOT a standalone-driver upper-tail radial-asymmetry effect -- it must originate in structure a copula on standalone margins cannot represent (nested inner-path joint dynamics).
